SURVEY REPORT: DRAINAGE INFRASTRUCTURE ISSUE IN CHENNAI 1. Title Assessment of Urban Drainage Conditions in Velachery, Chennai 2. Date of Survey The survey was conducted on 15 March 2026. 3. Geographical Area The study was carried out in Velachery, Chennai; a densely populated and low-lying urban locality to waterlogging: 4. Objective of the Survey The primary objective was to assess the condition of the drainage system, identify the nature and causes of recurring waterlogging, and evaluate its impact 0n residents 5. Methodology field-based survey was conducted over two using: Structured questionnaires distributed to households Direct observation of drainage conditions Informal interviews with local residents and shopkeepers total of 120 households and 15 small businesses were surveyed. Data was compiled and analyzed to identify patterns and severity. 6. Type of Issue prone days

The issue identified is clogged and inefficient stormwater drainage; primarily caused by solid waste accumulation and poor maintenance. 7. Findings and Observations 78% of respondents reported water stagnation lasting more than 48 hours after rainfall. 65 % observed that drains were frequently blocked with plastic and debris. Several drainage channels were found to be partially covered or encroached, reducing flow capacity: Overflow of wastewater into streets was observed in multiple locations. These findings indicate a systemic failure in drainage management: 8. What is the Issue The locality suffers from frequent waterlogging due to blocked drainage lines, leading to stagnant water accumulation Poor waste disposal practices and irregular cleaning of drains aggravate the problem, especially during the monsoon season: 9. Scale of Effect The issue affects approximately 300-400 households, along with nearby commercial establishments. The impact includes: Disrupted daily movement Property damage Increased mosquito breeding and health risks 10. Scale of Urgency The situation is categorized as high urgency, as it directly affects public health and safety Immediate intervention is required, particularly before the onset of heavy rainfall,

11. Volunteer Requirements To initiate corrective measures such as cleanup drives and awareness campaigns, approximately 15-20 volunteers are required: 12. Type of Volunteer Need The following types of volunteers are needed: Sanitation workers and cleanup volunteers for drain clearing Awareness volunteers for community education on waste disposal Coordination volunteers to liaise with municipal authorities 13. Causes of the Problem Improper waste disposal by residents Lack of regular maintenance by civic authorities Rapid urbanization without drainage upgrades Encroachment on natural water channels 14. Recommendations 1, Immediate cleaning and desilting of drainage systems 2. Installation of waste filters to prevent clogging 3. Regular monitoring and maintenance schedules 4. Public awareness campaigns on proper waste disposal 5. Long-term infrastructure improvement projects 15. Conclusion The survey highlights a critical drainage issue in Velachery that significantly affects the quality of life.
